# Manual Email Warmup Tracker & Templates

This tracker outlines the daily schedule, email templates, and logging system to warm up **`contact@lansem.in`** and **`contact@suriosity.in`** for free.

---

## 📋 The Rules of Manual Warmup

1.  **Use real active emails**: Send emails to your personal email accounts (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo) or ask business partners, friends, or family members to help.
2.  **Rescue from Spam (Crucial)**: If your email lands in their spam folder, they **must** open it, click **"Not Spam"** (or "Report as safe"), and move it back to the Inbox.
3.  **Engage in back-and-forth**: When you send an email, they should reply to it. You should then send one more reply back. This thread history is highly valued by email algorithms.
4.  **Use templates**: Do not send empty emails, random letters, or single words like "test". Use the natural-sounding email copy templates provided below.

## 💬 Copy-Paste Warmup Conversation Templates

Use these natural business conversation threads. Send the first message, have your recipient reply with the suggested answer, and then send a final quick reply.

### Thread 1: Project Alignment
*   **Email 1 (You)**:
    *   *Subject*: Quick check on timeline
    *   *Body*: Hi there, just wanted to check if we are still on track for the draft review this Friday? Let me know if you need any adjustments to the schedule. Best, Arnab.
*   **Email 2 (Recipient Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Hi Arnab, yes, we are on track! I should have it ready for you by Friday afternoon.
*   **Email 3 (You Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Sounds great. Look forward to reviewing it. Thank you!

### Thread 2: Quick Question
*   **Email 1 (You)**:
    *   *Subject*: Recommendation for accounting tools?
    *   *Body*: Hello, do you have a preference between Xero and QuickBooks for managing smaller client portfolios? Let me know what has worked best for your workflow. Thanks, Arnab.
*   **Email 2 (Recipient Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Hi Arnab, I highly prefer Xero for its bank reconciliation and clean user interface. It works wonders for client collaboration.
*   **Email 3 (You Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Appreciate the insights! I will check out Xero.

### Thread 3: Article Feedback
*   **Email 1 (You)**:
    *   *Subject*: Read your recent post
    *   *Body*: Hi, came across your recent article on sustainable sourcing. Loved the insights on supply chain transparency. Let's catch up sometime next week? Best, Arnab.
*   **Email 2 (Recipient Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Hi Arnab, thank you for reading it! I'd love to connect. I'm free on Wednesday morning.
*   **Email 3 (You Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Perfect, I'll send over a calendar invite shortly. Talk soon!

### Thread 4: Meeting Notes
*   **Email 1 (You)**:
    *   *Subject*: Meeting notes & next steps
    *   *Body*: Hello, thanks for the chat today. Here are the key action items: 1) Finalize domain setup 2) Start warmup schedule. Let me know if I missed anything. Best, Arnab.
*   **Email 2 (Recipient Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Hi Arnab, looks complete to me. I'll get started on my tasks tomorrow.
*   **Email 3 (You Reply)**:
    *   *Body*: Perfect, thanks!
